Scoring Guide

Safety Score

The score starts from a high baseline and is reduced by risk signals from chemicals, operating conditions, safeguards, stage risks, and scale-up impact. Higher score means lower overall route risk.

Risk Grade Thresholds

The final score is translated into four levels: Low, Moderate, High, and Critical, so users can quickly interpret route risk severity.

Confidence

Confidence reflects how complete your input data is. More complete process and hazard information leads to higher confidence in the screening result.

Risk Driver Breakdown

Each bar represents how strongly one risk factor contributes to score reduction. Longer bars indicate stronger risk-driving factors that should be prioritized for control.

Stage Risk Radar Mapping

The radar chart compares risk intensity across Charging, Reaction, Quench, and Isolation stages, helping identify where stage-specific safeguards should be strengthened first.

Safety-Confidence Position Map

The point combines Safety Score (Y-axis) and Confidence (X-axis). Green zones indicate robust and acceptable status, while red zones indicate poor or uncertain safety decisions.
